Core Concepts

Before we build, let's understand the 'what' and the 'why'.

What is a Dashboard?

A dashboard is a visual display of key data points, or "metrics." It helps you quickly understand performance, track progress, and make data-driven decisions.

Think of it like a car's dashboard: it shows you all the important information (speed, fuel, engine temp) at a glance.

Why Apache Superset?

  • Open-Source & Free: No cost to use, backed by a strong community.
  • Powerful: Connects to a vast array of modern databases.
  • Intuitive Interface: Designed for both technical and non-technical users.
  • Beautiful Visualizations: A wide range of chart types to choose from.

Step 1: Connecting Your Data

The first step is to tell Superset where your data lives. This involves adding your database as a source.

  • Navigate to Data > Databases.
  • Click the + button to add a new database.
  • Select your database type (e.g., PostgreSQL, MySQL, Google BigQuery).
  • Enter the connection details (host, port, username, password).
  • Pro Tip: Test the connection before saving!

Step 2: Exploring Your Data & Creating a Dataset

Once a database is connected, you need to specify which table or view you want to visualize. This is called a dataset.

  • Navigate to Data > Datasets.
  • Click + to create a new dataset from your connected database.
  • A dataset is your working table for creating charts.

Step 3: Building Your First Chart

This is where your data comes to life. Each chart is a single visualization built from a dataset.

  • Navigate to Charts and click + New Chart.
  • Choose your dataset and a visualization type (e.g., Bar Chart, Line Chart).
  • Click Create New Chart to enter the chart builder.

Understanding Chart Components

In the chart builder, you'll configure three main parts. Hover over each label below to learn more.

Metrics
Metrics
The values you want to measure (e.g., `SUM(sales)`, `AVG(price)`). These are the numbers that will determine the size of your bars, points on your lines, etc.
Dimensions
Dimensions
The categories you want to break your data down by (e.g., `product_category`, `country`). These are the labels along the axes of your chart.
Filters
Filters
How you narrow down your data to show a specific subset (e.g., `sales > 100`, `region = 'Europe'`). This helps focus your chart on the most relevant information.

Step 4: Assembling Your Dashboard

The final step is to bring all your charts together into a cohesive dashboard.

  • Navigate to Dashboards and click + Dashboard.
  • Give your dashboard a descriptive name. This creates a blank canvas.
  • Add charts by dragging them from the chart list or using the edit menu.
  • Arrange and resize charts by clicking and dragging them on the canvas. Organize them logically to tell a story with your data.
